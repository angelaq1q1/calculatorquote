Learn About Mean, Median, Mode, Range, and Midrange

The mean, median, and mode are basic ways to find the center of a dataset. The mean is the average of all numbers. Add the numbers and divide by the total count. For example, the mean of 2, 5, 6, 8, 9 is 6. The median is the middle number in a sorted list. If the dataset has an odd count, pick the center number. If even, take the average of the two middle numbers. For instance, the median of 2, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9 is 6.5. The mode is the number that appears most often. A dataset can have one mode or more. Using 2, 7, 6, 4, 7, 7, 6, 8, 7, 4, 6, the mode is 7.

Other measures include range and midrange. The range shows how spread out numbers are. Subtract the smallest number from the largest. The midrange is halfway between the minimum and maximum. Calculate it by adding them and dividing by two. These measures help you understand data quickly. The calculator shows all results automatically, making analysis fast and easy.

Q1: How to calculate mean mode and median?

A: Mean, Median, and Mode Formulas

1. Formula: Mean = (Sum of all values) ÷ (Number of values)

2. Mean = (Σ f × x) ÷ n.

3. Formula for even case: Median = ( (n/2)th value + ((n/2) + 1)th value ) ÷ 2.

Q2: What is the median of 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9?

A: Median is the middle number which divides the data into halves. This data has a total of 8 numbers in it, the data is already arranged so, middle to numbers is 5 and 6.

Q3: How to calculate the median?

A: The median is the middle value in a set of data. First, organize and order the data from smallest to largest. Divide the number of observations by two to find the midpoint value. Round the number up if there's an odd number of observations, and the value in that position is the median.

Q4: Is it better to use mean or median?

A: Median better when there are outliers. Mean is a parametric estimate (meaning) and is most useful when you know the shape of the distribution. Median is non-parametric (meaning) and there is no need for a distribution shape when used.

Q5: What if there are two modes?

A: After summarizing the data in a frequency table, you can see that there are two modes in this data set: 14 and 16. Both values appear 5 times in the data set and 5 is the highest frequency observed. The mode can't be used a measure of central tendency because there is more than one mode. It's a bimodal distribution.

Q6: What are the different types of modes?

A: Types of Mode

Type Meaning Example
Unimodal One mode 2, 3, 3, 5 (mode = 3)
