How the Triangle Area Calculator Works

The simplest formula you learned in school is:

area = 0.5 × base × height.

This works best when you know the height of the triangle.

If you don't know the height, don't worry. The calculator can still help. It uses different formulas depending on what you have:

Triangle with three sides (SSS):

If you know all three sides, use Heron's formula:

area = 0.25 × √((a + b + c) × (-a + b + c) × (a - b + c) × (a + b - c))

This works well when only the sides are given, like in engineering or surveying tasks.

Triangle with two sides and the angle between them (SAS):

If you know two sides and the angle in between, the calculator uses:

area = 0.5 × a × b × sin(γ)

Triangle with two angles and one side (ASA):

When you know two angles and a side, you can use:

area = a² × sin(β) × sin(γ) / (2 × sin(β + γ))

No matter what information you enter, the Triangle Area Calculator picks the right formula automatically. You don't need to remember any equations. It calculates everything for you in seconds.

❓Frequently Asked Questions About Triangle Area Calculator

Q1: How do you find the area of a triangle?

A: Area of a Triangle = A = ½ (b × h) square units where b and h are the base and height of the triangle, respectively.

Q2: How do you find the area of irregular shapes?

A:

  1. Break the shape into pieces that form the irregular shape.
  2. Find the area of the individual shapes.
  3. Add the areas together.

Q3: How is the area of an irregular quadrilateral calculated?

A:

  1. Divide the quadrilateral into separate triangles.
  2. Solve for the area of these triangles using any three methods: Side Angle Side (SAS), Law of Cosines, or Heron's Formula.
  3. Sum of the areas of triangles = Final area of an irregular quadrilateral.

Q4: How to calculate area of irregular shapes 3 sided?

A: An irregular triangle means a triangle whose sides are different in length. According to the Heron's formula: Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)], where, a, b and c are the sides of the triangle, and 's' is the semi-perimeter of the triangle.

Q5: Do triangles add up to 180 or 360?

A: The sum of the interior angle measures of a triangle always adds up to 180°.

Q6: What does delta △ mean?

A: In math, both uppercase (Δ) and lowercase delta (δ) mostly represent change or difference. The versatility of this symbol allows mathematicians to use it as a marker for variations in quantities, functions, or geometric properties.
