‚ùì FAQ ‚Äì Common Questions About Dog Sitting Costs
Q: How much does dog sitting cost per hour?
A: Dog sitting costs range from $15-25 per hour depending on dog size. Small dogs cost $15/hr, medium dogs $20/hr, and large dogs $25/hr. Additional factors like service type and location affect the final price.
Q: Do dog sitters charge extra for multiple dogs?
A: Yes, most professional dog sitters charge multipliers for multiple dogs. 2 dogs = 1.5x base rate, 3+ dogs = 2x base rate. Our calculator automatically includes these adjustments.
Q: What's the difference between dog walking and dog sitting?
A: Dog walking is typically shorter duration (1-2 hours) focused on exercise. Dog sitting includes longer care periods with feeding, bathroom breaks, and general supervision. Overnight care provides 24/7 supervision.
Q: Are dog sitting prices higher in major cities?
A: Yes, major cities typically charge 20% more due to higher operating costs and demand. Our calculator includes location-based pricing adjustments for high, medium, and low cost areas.
Q: What additional services do dog sitters offer?
A: Common additional services include medication administration ($3/day), bathing ($10/session), grooming, training reinforcement, and special dietary needs. These services are charged separately from base rates.
Q: How can I find reliable dog sitters near me?
A: Search online for "dog sitters near me" or use apps like Rover, Wag, or Care.com. Ask your veterinarian, local pet stores, or neighbors for recommendations. Check reviews, verify insurance, and meet the sitter before hiring.
Q: How can I save money on dog sitting costs?
A: Consider shorter duration services, fewer additional services, or hiring a neighborhood teenager for basic care. Group sitting (if your dog is social) can also reduce costs. Our calculator helps you explore different cost scenarios.
