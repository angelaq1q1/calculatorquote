❓ FAQ – Get the Right Tank Size and Keep Your Fish Healthy

Q1: How do I calculate the volume of my fish tank?
A: To calculate your fish tank volume, multiply the length × width × height of your tank. Use our calculator above for accurate results in both liters and gallons. For example: a 100cm × 50cm × 40cm tank = 200,000 cm³ = 200 liters ≈ 53 US gallons.

Q2: How many gallons aquarium calculator?
A: Our aquarium calculator automatically converts your tank dimensions to gallons. Simply enter your tank's length, width, and height in any unit (cm, inches, meters, or feet), and we'll show you the volume in both liters and US gallons. This helps you determine the right tank size for your fish.

Q3: What's the ideal fish capacity for my aquarium?
A: As a general rule, allow 1 gallon (3.8 liters) of water per inch of fish length. Our calculator provides fish capacity recommendations based on your tank volume. Remember to consider fish behavior, adult size, and swimming space requirements when stocking your aquarium.

Q4: How do I choose the right filter for my tank?
A: Your filter should circulate the entire tank volume 3-5 times per hour. Our calculator recommends filter flow rates based on your tank size. For example, a 50-gallon tank needs a filter rated for 150-250 gallons per hour (GPH) for optimal water circulation and filtration.
