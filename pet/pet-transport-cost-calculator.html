❓ FAQ – because pet parents have questions

Q1: How much does pet transport cost?
A: Pet transport costs typically range from $200-$2,500 depending on distance, pet size, and transport method. Ground transport costs $0.50-$1.50 per mile plus base fees, while air transport ranges from $200-$500 for domestic flights to $500-$2,000 for international. Additional services like pickup, health checks, and insurance add $50-$300 to the total cost.

Q2: What is the cheapest way to transport a pet?
A: Ground transport is usually the cheapest way to transport pets for distances under 500 miles. DIY ground transport with your own vehicle costs only gas and time, while professional ground transport services cost $0.50-$1.50 per mile. For longer distances, air transport becomes more cost-effective, especially for smaller pets that can travel in the cabin.

Q3: How do I ship my pet safely?
A: To ship your pet safely, choose a reputable pet transport service, ensure proper health documentation, use an appropriate travel crate, and consider pet insurance. For air travel, book direct flights when possible, avoid extreme weather conditions, and provide familiar items in the crate. Always verify the transport company's credentials and read reviews from other pet owners.

Q4: Do I need a health certificate for pet transport?
A: Yes, most airlines and professional pet transport services require a health certificate issued by a licensed veterinarian within 10 days of travel. The certificate must confirm your pet is healthy, up-to-date on vaccinations, and free from contagious diseases. International transport may require additional documentation and quarantine periods depending on the destination country.

Q5: Can I transport my pet in the cabin?
A: Small pets (typically under 20 pounds) can often travel in the cabin with you on most airlines for an additional fee of $75-$200. Pets must remain in an airline-approved carrier that fits under the seat. Larger pets must travel in the cargo hold. Each airline has specific size and weight restrictions, so check with your chosen carrier before booking.

Q6: What documents do I need for international pet transport?
A: International pet transport requires a health certificate, proof of current vaccinations (especially rabies), microchip identification, and often an import permit from the destination country. Some countries require blood tests, quarantine periods, or specific vaccination schedules. Contact the destination country's embassy or agricultural department for exact requirements, as regulations vary significantly between countries.
