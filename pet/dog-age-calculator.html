Q1: How to calculate a dog's age?
A: As a general guideline, the American Veterinary Medical Association breaks the calculations down like this:
1. 15 human years equals the first year of a medium-sized dog's life.
2. Year two for a dog equals about nine years for a human.
3. And after that, each human year would be approximately five years for a dog.

Q2: Can a dog live 20 years?
A: The smaller breeds of dogs tend to live the longest. Yorkshire terriers, Chihuahuas, Dachshunds, Toy Poodles and Lhasa Apsos are the breeds who typically live the longest with these averaging a lifespan of up to 20 years. This is much higher than the average lifespan of a dog which is between 10 and 13 years.

Q3: Do small dogs live longer?
A: In fact, research consistently shows us that smaller breeds typically outlive larger ones. Researchers at the University of Washington studied over 25,000 dogs and 238 breeds. They found that, on average, small dogs do tend to live longer.

Q4: What word makes dogs calm?
A: Rather than sit, a watch, steady, focus or chill command might be used. Similarly, instead of down a relax, settle, or SOFT command might be considered, while follow or heel should be used for a calm, loose leash walk, and go to your mat should mean go settle down in your bed.

Q5: How long should I grieve my dog?
A: How long does grief last after losing a pet? Sadly, there isn't a timetable for when things will begin to feel easier. Q6: How do you honor a dog's memory?
A: Consider creating a special place outside in their memory. Put their name on a garden stone and add it to your garden, plant a tree in their honor, or have their name engraved on a bench. If you keep your pet's remains, you can also bury them in your yard and mark their resting place in one of these ways.
