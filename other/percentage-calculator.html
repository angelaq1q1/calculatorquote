Understanding Percentage

A percentage is part of a whole. It is a way to express a number as a fraction of 100, and it is denoted using the percent symbol %.

Calculating percentage allows you to compare ratios when sets are different sizes. If you see an example of 25% it means 25 out of 100, or a quarter of the whole.

Common Percentage Calculations

Basic Percentage Calculations

1. What is P% of X?

Formula: Y = (P/100) × X

Example: What is 15% of $80? Answer: 15/100 × $80 = $12

2. Y is what % of X?

Formula: P = (Y/X) × 100

Example: 15 is what percent of 60? Answer: (15/60) × 100 = 25%

3. Y is P% of what?

Formula: X = Y ÷ (P/100)

Example: 20 is 40% of what? Answer: 20 ÷ (40/100) = 50

Percentage Addition and Subtraction

1. X plus P%

Formula: Result = X + (X × P/100)

Example: 100 plus 20% = 100 + (100 × 20/100) = 120

2. X minus P%

Formula: Result = X - (X × P/100)

Example: 100 minus 20% = 100 - (100 × 20/100) = 80
