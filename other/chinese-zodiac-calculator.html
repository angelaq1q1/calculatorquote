🐲 Chinese Zodiac FAQs — Your Complete Guide

Q1: What is the Chinese zodiac?
A: The Chinese zodiac is a 12-year cycle where each year is represented by an animal sign. It's based on the lunar calendar and is used to determine personality traits, compatibility, and fortune predictions in Chinese culture.

Q2: How is my Chinese zodiac sign determined?
A: Your Chinese zodiac sign is determined by your birth year. The system follows a 12-year cycle, so people born 12 years apart share the same animal sign. The calculation is: (Birth Year % 12).

Q3: What are the 12 Chinese zodiac animals?
A: The 12 animals in order are: Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, and Pig. Each animal represents different personality traits and characteristics.

Q4: How accurate is Chinese zodiac compatibility?
A: Chinese zodiac compatibility is based on traditional astrology principles and should be taken as general guidance rather than absolute truth. Individual relationships depend on many factors beyond zodiac signs.

Q5: Can I have more than one zodiac sign?
A: No, you have one primary Chinese zodiac sign based on your birth year. However, some systems also consider the month and day of birth for more detailed analysis.

Q6: What's the difference between Chinese and Western zodiac?
A: The Chinese zodiac is based on a 12-year cycle with animals, while the Western zodiac is based on a 12-month cycle with constellations. Chinese zodiac focuses on the year of birth, while Western zodiac focuses on the month of birth.
