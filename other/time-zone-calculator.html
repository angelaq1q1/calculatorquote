About Time Zones

A time zone is a region on Earth that uses a uniform standard time. Time zones are generally based on the mean solar time of a particular meridian located in the middle of that zone. Most time zones are exactly one hour apart, and by convention compute their local time as an offset from UTC (Coordinated Universal Time).

Time zones are usually defined by the boundaries of countries and their subdivisions rather than strictly by longitude, because it is convenient for areas in frequent communication to keep the same time. Many time zones are adjusted by one hour during summer time (also known as "daylight saving time").

Note: This calculator does not account for daylight saving time (DST). The times shown are based on standard time zones only.
