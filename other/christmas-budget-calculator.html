🎁 Frequently Asked Questions About Christmas Budgeting

Q1: What's the average Christmas budget?
A: Americans plan to spend $997.79 on holiday purchases for themselves and their families this year. That's down from last year's $1,048, but it's still a big chunk of change. Yeah, Christmas can get expensive. But remember: Those are averages based on what the average American plans to spend.

Q2: What is the 5 gift rule for Christmas?
A: The five gift rule consists of giving something they want, something they need, something to wear, something to read, and something to experience. If you've not heard of the Five Gift Rule, it's based upon the principle of quality over quantity.

Q3: How much is normal to spend on a child for Christmas?
A: How much to spend per child on Christmas gifts? Parents expect to spend $461 per child, on average, this Christmas. On the high end, 9% of parents said they will spend $1,000 or more per child. And on the low end, 4% said they'll keep it under $100.

Q4: How many gifts does the average child get at Christmas?
A: There is no magic number but the general consensus seems to be between three gifts potentially up to around five. There will be many factors that contribute to this decisions; how many children you have, presents other family members may buy, how old your children are and, of course, budget.

Q5: Is it still Christmas after 25?
A: In Western Christianity, the Christmas season is traditionally synonymous with Christmastide, which runs from December 25 (Christmas Day) to January 5 (Twelfth Night or Epiphany Eve), popularly known as the 12 Days of Christmas.

Q6: How to budget for xmas?
A: Tips for keeping family Christmas costs down
1. Set your budget. From decorations and travel to presents and festive food, there's a lot to buy.
2. Write a list.
3. Buy second hand.
4. Make Christmas decorations.
5. Get your Christmas dinner for less.
6. Make the most of your leftovers.
7. Borrow, don't buy.
8. Say no to nursery/preschool collections.
9. Get cheaper travel
10. Ask for help
