Why Does This Biblical Date Calculator Not Use the Modern Gregorian Calendar?

According to historical records summarized by Wikipedia, the Gregorian calendar is the civil dating system used by most of the world today. It was introduced in 1582 by Pope Gregory XIII to correct the inaccuracy of the earlier Julian calendar. The main improvement was an adjusted leap-year rule, which made the average year 365.2425 days‚Äîmuch closer to the real solar year of about 365.2422 days.

However, this also explains why many biblical date calculations cannot rely on the modern January‚ÄìDecember system. The Bible does not use the Gregorian calendar. Instead, most biblical timing is connected to the Hebrew (Biblical) calendar, which follows a very different structure.

The Biblical (Hebrew) Calendar

The Biblical calendar is lunar-solar. Months begin with the new moon, while the year stays aligned with agricultural seasons.

First Month: Nisan (Abib), beginning in spring when the barley ripens (Exodus 12:2; 13:4)

Normal Structure: 12 months, with an occasional additional month to keep festivals in their proper seasons

Key Observances: Passover (Nisan 14), biblical feasts, and sacred days

This system connects faith, nature, worship, and agricultural life, making time measurement part of spiritual practice rather than only a civil function.

The Gregorian Calendar

The Gregorian calendar is completely different in its design and purpose.

Basis: Solar only, with leap years to maintain accuracy

Origin: Introduced by Pope Gregory XIII as a correction to the Julian system

First Month: January, named after the Roman god Janus

Usage: Modern global civil calendar

Simple Summary

The Biblical calendar is rooted in faith, sacred observances, and God-appointed seasons.

The Gregorian calendar is a precise civil system created for international timekeeping.

Because of this major difference, biblical date tools like this calculator rely on Biblical/Hebrew calendar principles rather than the standard Gregorian system, helping you get results that are closer to the context of Scripture and historical practice.

Q1: How can you calculate your due date?
A: You can calculate your pregnancy due date by counting 40 weeks (280 days) from the first day of your last menstrual period. There are also online pregnancy calculators that can do this automatically.

Q2: How to calculate your next period date?
A: To estimate your next period, add your average menstrual cycle length (usually 28 days) to the first day of your last period. This gives you an approximate date for your next cycle.

Q3: How to calculate the difference in dates in Excel?
A: In Excel, you can calculate the difference between two dates using the formula: =End_Date - Start_Date. This will return the number of days between the dates.

Q6: What calendar did God use in the Bible?
A: God's calendar in the Bible, also known as the Biblical or Hebrew calendar, is a lunisolar system established at Creation (Genesis 1:14) using the sun and moon for signs, seasons (appointed times or moedim), days, and years, later formalized with Israel's Exodus, marking time by lunar months (new moon to new moon) and solar cycles (seasons), with key holy days (feasts) like Passover, Pentecost, and Tabernacles pointing to Christ's work and prophetic future.
