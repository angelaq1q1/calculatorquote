Q1: How do I find out my ring size?

A: To find your ring size, wrap string or paper around the base of your finger, mark the overlap, measure the length in millimeters, and use that measurement (circumference) on a ring size chart to find the corresponding size; for best accuracy, measure your knuckle too and visit a jeweler, or measure an existing ring's inner diameter.

Method 1: Using String or Paper (Finger Circumference)

  1. Wrap: Take a strip of paper or non-stretchy string and wrap it snugly around the base of the finger you'll wear the ring on.
  2. Mark: Mark the point where the paper or string overlaps with a pen.
  3. Measure: Lay the strip flat and measure the length in millimeters (mm) from the start to the mark.
  4. Calculate Diameter (Optional but helpful): Divide the length by 3.14 (Pi) to get the diameter.
  5. Find Size: Use a ring size chart (found online or in stores) to match your circumference or diameter to a standard ring size.

Method 2: Using an Existing Ring (Inner Diameter)

  1. Select Ring: Choose a ring that fits the intended finger comfortably.
  2. Measure: Place the ring on a ruler and measure the inside diameter (the widest part of the hole) in millimeters.
  3. Check Chart: Match the diameter measurement to a ring size chart to find your size.

Tips for Accuracy

  • Measure when your hands are at a normal temperature (not too hot or cold).
  • Measure your knuckle as well; if it's significantly larger, you might need a slightly larger size to get it on, says Daniel Wellington.
  • For the most precise fit, visit a jeweler for a professional sizing.
