🛡️ Password Generator FAQs: Stay Safe and Smart Online

Q1: What is a good random password?
A: Use at least 16 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across multiple online accounts to keep yourself safer if a security breach strikes.

Q2: What are random passwords?
A: A random password generator is a software program, hardware device, or online tool that automatically generates a password using parameters that a user sets, including mixed-case letters, numbers, symbols, pronounceability, length, and strength.

Q3: What is a 12 strong password?
A: Password security starts with creating a strong password. A strong password is: At least 12 characters long but 14 or more is better. A combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

Q4: What is a dummy password?
A: "some value that syntactically fits the requirements of a regular password including matching the length of the replaced password but is not actually the password to be used in this exchange"

Q5: Is a 4 word password strong?
A: We recommend that you use passphrases, as they are longer and easier to remember than a password made up of random, mixed characters. A passphrase is a memorized phrase consisting of a sequence of mixed words with or without spaces. Your passphrase should be at least 4 words and 15 characters in length.

Q6: How often should password be changed?
A: For most accounts, changing passwords every 3-6 months is recommended. However, change immediately if you suspect a breach, and for high-security accounts (banking, email), consider changing every 2-3 months. The key is using strong, unique passwords rather than frequent changes of weak ones.
