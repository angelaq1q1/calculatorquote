❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: 🧽 How do I remove mold myself?
A: To remove mold naturally, mix equal parts water and white vinegar in a spray bottle. Apply to affected areas, let it sit for an hour, then scrub and rinse. You can also use baking soda mixed with water for scrubbing. Ensure proper ventilation to prevent mold regrowth.

Q2: ⚠️ What are the 10 warning signs of mold toxicity?
A: Although symptoms can vary, the most common symptoms seen in people exposed to mold indoors include: Nasal and sinus congestion, Eye irritation, such as itchy, red, watery eyes, Wheezing and difficulty breathing, Cough, Throat irritation, Skin irritation, such as a rash, Headache.

Q3: ❌ Can mold ever be fully removed?
A: Mold can grow on or fill in the empty spaces and crevices of porous materials, so the mold may be difficult or impossible to remove completely.

Q4: 🌿 What plant removes 78% of airborne mold?
A: English Ivy has been shown to eliminate up to 78% of airborne mold in 12 hours, making it ideal for allergy-prone areas like bathrooms or basements.

Q5: 👷 How do professionals remove mold?
A: Mold remediation experts use a multi-step approach that includes: 1. HEPA vacuuming to remove loose spores, 2. Antimicrobial treatments to kill mould at the root level, 3. ULV (Ultra Low Volume) fogging to disinfect and remove airborne spores, 4. Encapsulation techniques to prevent future regrowth.

Q6: 🎨 Can you paint over mold?
A: The short answer: No, you absolutely shouldn't paint over mold. Even though it's tempting to cover a moldy spot with a fresh coat of paint, it will not solve the problem. Mold is a living organism, and painting over mold just hides the issue without stopping the growth underneath.

Q7: 🍶 Will spraying vinegar in the shower prevent mold?
A: Plus, it is great at preventing mold in the first place. Vinegar contains no toxic fumes like many other agents used to kill mold, such as bleach. Pour mild white vinegar into a spray bottle, do not dilute with water. Vinegar's acidic qualities make it quite deadly to mold, that's why you don't want to water it down.
