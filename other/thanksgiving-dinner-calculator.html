Q1: How much turkey do I need per person?
A: Generally, you need about 1 pound of turkey per adult and 0.5 pounds per child. If you want leftovers, add about 30% more. For example, 8 adults and 4 children would need about 10 pounds of turkey, or 13 pounds if you want leftovers.

Q2: What's the best way to calculate Thanksgiving portions?
A: Start with the number of adults and children, then consider appetites and whether you want leftovers. Children typically eat about half as much as adults. Plan for 1-1.5 pounds of turkey per adult, and adjust other dishes proportionally.

Q3: How do I plan for unexpected guests?
A: It's always smart to plan for 10-15% more guests than expected. Keep some extra rolls, sides, and desserts on hand. You can also ask guests to bring a side dish to help with the planning and costs.

Q4: What's in a traditional Thanksgiving dinner?
A: Many of the dishes in a traditional Thanksgiving dinner are made from ingredients native to the Americas, including the turkey bird, potato, sweet potato, corn (maize), squash (including pumpkin), green bean, and cranberry.
