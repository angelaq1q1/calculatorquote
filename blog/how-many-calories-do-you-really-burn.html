How Many Calories Do You Really Burn? Walking vs Burpees vs Squats vs Running vs Stairs vs Planks

Everyone wants to burn calories efficiently, but not all exercises are equal. Walking is low-impact and steady, while burpees are intense and explosive. Squats build strength while burning calories, and running improves endurance. Stair climbing challenges your legs and heart, and planks strengthen your core. Using this guide, you'll see real calorie comparisons, get access to interactive calculators, and learn ready-to-use weekly workout plans. By the end, you'll know exactly which exercises fit your goals and how to structure a routine that maximizes fat burn and strength gains.

Walking: Low-Impact Fat Burner

Walking is simple, accessible, and surprisingly effective. Even a 30-minute brisk walk can burn a noticeable number of calories. Using a walking calorie calculator, you can estimate your energy expenditure based on weight, pace, and incline.

Benefits of walking:

  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Supports weight management
  • Low impact on joints

Pro tips:

  • Brisk walking burns 30–50% more calories than casual strolling.
  • Incline or stairs increase intensity without extra impact.
  • Track daily steps and pace for consistent progress.

Example: A 70kg person walking briskly for 30 minutes burns ~150 kcal, while walking uphill can reach ~220 kcal.

Burpees: Full-Body Explosion

Burpees combine squat, plank, and jump in one fluid motion. They are high-intensity and excellent for short, effective calorie burns.

Key points:

  • Burns ~10–15 kcal per minute for a 70kg person
  • Engages chest, arms, core, glutes, quads
  • Ideal for HIIT sessions

Modifications: Beginners can step back instead of jumping to plank. Advanced users can add push-ups or tuck jumps.

Example Calculation: 10 burpees (~1 min) burns ~12 kcal for a 70kg person. 100 burpees (~10 min) can burn ~120 kcal.

Pro tip: Combine burpees with other bodyweight moves for circuit workouts. Use our burpee calorie calculator to track your exact burn.

Squats: Strength and Calorie Burn

Squats are perfect for lower-body strength and calorie burn. They target glutes, quads, hamstrings, and core.

Benefits:

  • Improves balance and posture
  • Increases functional strength
  • Can add weights for higher intensity

Example:

  • 20 bodyweight squats (~1 min) burn ~8–10 kcal (70kg person)
  • 20 jump squats (~1 min) burn ~14–16 kcal

Tips:

  • Focus on form to maximize benefits
  • Perform multiple sets for steady calorie burn
  • Gradually increase difficulty

Calculate your exact burn with our squat calorie calculator.

Running: Classic Calorie Burner

Running is a go-to cardio exercise for endurance and calorie burn. Using a running calorie calculator, you can estimate burn based on distance, pace, and weight.

Key points:

  • 5 km run (~30 min at 10 km/h) burns ~300 kcal for 70kg person
  • Sprint intervals burn more than steady jogging
  • Good form and progressive mileage reduce injury risk

Pro tip: Combine with strength exercises (squats, planks) to maximize total daily energy expenditure.

Stair Climbing: Leg and Heart Power

Stair climbing is a low-cost, highly effective exercise. It strengthens legs, glutes, and cardiovascular fitness.

Example:

  • 10 minutes climbing stairs (~70 steps/min) burns ~90–110 kcal for 70kg person
  • Adding dumbbells or increasing speed boosts burn

Pro tip: Integrate stairs into daily routines or use them as a cardio finisher. Track your progress with our stair climbing calorie calculator.

Planks: Core Strength Meets Fat Burn

Planks primarily target the core, but shoulders, back, and legs are also engaged.

Example:

  • 1 min plank burns ~3–5 kcal (70kg person)
  • Side planks or plank-to-push-up variations burn more

Tips:

  • Incorporate planks daily for posture and stability
  • Gradually increase hold time for more calorie burn and strength

Use our plank calorie calculator to see how different variations affect your burn.

Putting It All Together: Create Your Calorie-Burning Routine

Balancing cardio and strength exercises ensures efficient calorie burn. Use interactive calculators to track your energy expenditure for walking, burpees, squats, running, stairs, and planks.

  • Mix low-impact exercises (walking, planks) with high-intensity ones (burpees, sprinting)
  • Track calories by weight, duration, and exercise type
  • Use weekly templates to stay consistent
  • Gradually increase intensity, repetitions, or duration

Example: A 70kg person following the Beginner Fat-Burn Plan burns ~1,300–1,500 kcal/week, complementing a healthy diet.
