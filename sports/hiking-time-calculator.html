How the Hiking Time Calculator Works 🧗

Our Hiking Time Calculator estimates your total hiking time by applying the classic Naismith Rule, with corrections for terrain and descent. Here's how it works:

Distance: Enter the total trail length in miles or kilometers. Naismith's Rule suggests 1 hour for every 3 miles (5 km) of flat terrain.

Elevation Gain: Input total ascent in feet or meters. The calculator adds 1 hour for every 2,000 feet (600 meters) of uphill.

Descent: Enter the total downhill. For gentle slopes (5–12°), subtract 10 minutes per 300 meters. For steeper slopes (>12°), add 10 minutes per 300 meters.

Ground Conditions: Choose the terrain type. On roads or easy paths, the pace is 3 mph (5 km/h). On rough ground, it slows to 2.5 mph (4 km/h).

Break Time: Include any planned rest breaks in minutes.

Formula:

Total Time = (Distance ÷ Walking Speed) + (Elevation Gain ÷ 2,000 × 60) + (Descent Correction) + Break Time

This method provides a reliable estimate for planning your hike, helping you finish safely and on time 🥾.

Hiking Time Calculator FAQ — Plan Your Trail Safely and Easily 🧗

Q1: What is hiking time?

A: Keep in mind that hikes are measured in total roundtrip distance, therefore a 5 mile roundtrip hike will be 2.5 miles up and 2.5 miles down. For an idea of how long it takes to do a hike, consider that the average time it takes to walk a mile is 20 minutes, give or take a bit.

Q2: What is the hiking time rule?

A: The original Naismith's rule from 1892 says that one should allow one hour per three miles on the map and an additional hour per 2000 feet of ascent. It is included in the last sentence of his report from a trip. The basic rule assumes hikers of reasonable fitness, on typical terrain, and under normal conditions.

Q3: How to calculate hiking time?

A: One of the most simple and well-known ways to calculate hiking time is Naismith's Rule. So a 6-mile hike with 2,000 feet in elevation gain should take 3 hours (2hrs + 1 hr). In the field, it might be easier to use (20 minutes * # of miles) +15 minutes for every 500 feet ascended – 15 minutes for every 500 descended.

Q4: How long is a 1 hour hike?

A: Naismith's rule: Allow 1 hour for every 3 miles (5 km) forward + 1 hour for every 2000 feet (600 m) of ascent. This means that the average walking time for a mile according to Naismith is 20 minutes.

Q5: How many km per hour hiking?

A: Properly estimate the length of your hike before you leave. The average speed of travel on a nature trail is 2 to 2.5 km per hour for a beginner. First time hiker? Opt for shorter trails with little elevation gain.

Q6: What does long distance hiking do to your body?

A: The combination of walking for extended periods and carrying a backpack provides a full-body workout that enhances cardiovascular health, strengthens muscles, and improves endurance. It also promotes weight loss and the varied terrain can challenge your balance and agility.
