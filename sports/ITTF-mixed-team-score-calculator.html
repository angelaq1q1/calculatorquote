Below is the complete competition format, match structure, and scoring rules.

Overall Match System

Each team match uses a 15-game, first-to-8-wins format.

A match contains five events, and each event is played as a best-of-three (BO3) match:

  • Mixed Doubles (always the opening event)
  • Women's Singles
  • Men's Singles
  • Women's Doubles or Men's Doubles (chosen by the trailing team)
  • Remaining doubles event
  • The winner of each event earns 2 games.
  • The losing side earns 0 games.
  • Teams accumulate games across all events.
  • As soon as one team reaches 8 total games, the match ends immediately.
  • Each player may participate in a maximum of two events per team match.

This system rewards lineup depth, tactical planning, and strategic use of doubles players.

Stage One ‚Äî Group Stage (16 Teams)

16 teams are divided into 4 groups, each containing 4 teams.

Groups play a round-robin format (3 matches per team).

Ranking rules:

  • Match wins
  • Game difference
  • Head-to-head
  • Total games won
  • Points difference (if needed)

The top two teams from each group (8 total) advance to Stage Two.

Stage Two ‚Äî Large Round Robin (8 Teams)

The eight qualified teams enter a single large round-robin league.

Stage Three ‚Äî Knockout Finals

The top four teams enter a single-elimination bracket:

  • Semifinal 1: Rank 1 vs Rank 4
  • Semifinal 2: Rank 2 vs Rank 3
  • Final: Winners of semifinals
  • Third-place match: Losers of semifinals

All knockout matches still use the 15-game, first-to-8-wins system.

This entire knockout structure mirrors the newly adopted Olympic Mixed Team format, ensuring full consistency between World Cup competition and LA28 event requirements.

üèìFrequently Asked Questions ‚Äì ITTF Mixed Team World Cup

Q1. What is the ITTF Mixed Team World Cup format?

A: The ITTF Mixed Team World Cup uses a three-stage structure: Stage One: 16 teams in four groups; Stage Two: 8-team large round robin with carried-over results; Stage Three: Knockout semifinals, final, and third-place match. Each team match follows a 15-game, first-to-8-wins system with five BO3 events.

Q2. How does scoring work in a Mixed Team match?

A: Every event is played as a best-of-three match. The winning team earns 2 games, and the losing team earns 0 games. Games accumulate across events. The first team to reach 8 total games wins the match immediately.

Q3. Why is the Mixed Team World Cup format used in the Olympics?

A: Starting from 2024‚Äì2025, the ITTF format was officially integrated into the Olympic team table tennis system. The format was chosen for three reasons: Balanced gender representation; More strategic lineup choices; Faster, more exciting matches for global audiences.

Q4. What does "the trailing team chooses the next event" mean?

A: After the first three fixed events (Mixed Doubles, Women's Singles, Men's Singles), the team that is behind in total games gets to choose whether to play Women's Doubles or Men's Doubles next. This tactical flexibility adds momentum swings and deeper strategic planning.

Q5. Can a player compete in multiple events within the same match?

A: Yes ‚Äî but with limitations. Each player may play a maximum of 2 events per team match. This rule ensures lineup balance and prevents over-reliance on a single star player.

Q6. What is the purpose of carrying over results in Stage Two?

A: In Stage Two, teams do not replay matches from Stage One against teams from the same group. Instead, those results are carried over. This ensures that every group match matters, even early ones, and rewards consistent performance across the entire tournament.
