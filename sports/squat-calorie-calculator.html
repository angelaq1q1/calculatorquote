Squats are one of the simplest ways to train your legs and burn calories fast — but most people have no idea how many calories they actually burn. This Squat Calorie Calculator helps you instantly estimate your calorie burn based on reps, duration, weight, and workout intensity. How the Calculator Works + What Influences Your Results

Your calorie burn isn't determined by reps alone. The calculation uses a combination of personal data and activity factors to estimate calories burned from squats. The logic includes:

Body Weight

Heavier individuals burn more calories because their bodies require more energy to move. The calculator uses a weight-based multiplier to get an accurate result.

Number of Squats or Duration

You can enter total reps or total minutes. Higher volume naturally increases total squat calories burned.

Workout Intensity

Slow, controlled squats burn fewer calories compared to high-intensity sessions like HIIT or air-squat speed sets. The tool adjusts calorie burn using an intensity factor.

MET Formula Integration

Squats generally fall within a 5.0–8.0 MET range depending on speed and form. The calculator applies this MET value to estimate energy expenditure:

Calories = MET × Weight (kg) × Duration (hours)

When This Tool Is Most Useful

  • After completing a workout and wanting an immediate calorie estimate.
  • Comparing how many calories you burned doing 50 vs. 100 squats.
  • Tracking daily or weekly squat progress as part of a fat-loss plan.
  • Planning workouts based on a calorie-burn target.

Important Notes

  • Squats alone won't create rapid fat loss — consistency matters.
  • Poor form reduces calorie burn and increases injury risk.
  • Adding weights significantly raises calorie output.
  • Your actual burn may vary slightly because metabolism and muscle efficiency differ from person to person.

❓Frequently Asked Questions About Squat Calories

Q1: How many calories are in a squat?
A: Squats typically burn about 8 calories for every minute of exercise, but this number can vary based on various elements such as body weight, intensity, and muscle mass. Different squat variations can also alter the number of calories burned during workouts.
Q2: How many squats equal 10,000 steps?
A: 10 squats every 45 minutes is equal to 10, 000 steps per day and new scientific study found. When your muscles are contracted, they produce very important compounds for your brain, your metabolism, and even the fat burning process. A simple walk does not have the same effect.
Q3: Is squat good for losing weight?
A: Squats burn calories and might help you lose weight. They also lower your chances of injuring your knees and ankles. As you exercise, the movement strengthens your tendons, bones, and ligaments around the leg muscles. It takes some of the weight off your knees and ankles.
Q4: What should I eat after squats?
A: Good post-workout food choices include:
* Yogurt and fruit.
* Peanut butter sandwich.
* Low-fat chocolate milk and pretzels.
* Post-workout recovery smoothie.
* Turkey on whole-grain bread with vegetables.
Q5: What exercise burns the most calories?
A: If you want to get the most calorie bang for your buck, you might want to take up running. Running burns the most calories per hour. But if running isn't your thing, there are other calorie-burning exercises like HIIT workouts, jumping rope, and swimming.
Q6: Why do I feel so good after squats?
A: Strength training exercises like squats can help strengthen and tone the muscles in your lower body. When these muscles are in good condition, you may find that you can move more comfortably, with less pain, and that everything from walking to bending to exercising is easier to do.
