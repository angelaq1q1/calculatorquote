Why Snow Shoveling Counts as Exercise

Shoveling snow engages multiple muscle groups. Your arms, shoulders, back, and core all work together. Even using an electric snow shovel, you still get a moderate workout. Shoveling helps improve strength, endurance, and cardiovascular fitness.

Tracking burning calories shoveling snow is useful if you want to monitor daily activity or include chores as part of a fitness routine. It also motivates you to pace yourself. Take regular breaks, stay hydrated, and don't push yourself too hard. Safety is just as important as calories burned.

Tips for a Safer, More Effective Snow Clearing

  • Warm up for a few minutes before you start.
  • Use correct lifting techniques to protect your back.
  • Dress in layers to stay warm but avoid overheating.
  • Switch hands or shovel sides periodically to balance muscle use.
  • Consider a snow shovel with wheels or an electric snow shovel to reduce strain.

Following these tips not only keeps you safe but also helps maximize your calorie burn.

❓Frequently Asked Questions About Snow Shoveling Calories

Q1: How many calories do 30 minutes of snow shoveling burn
A: Shoveling snow for 30 minutes burns roughly 180 to 270 calories, depending on your weight and effort, with a 155-pound person burning around 220-223 calories, making it a great full-body workout that strengthens legs, core, arms, and back. Heavier individuals burn more (e.g., 266 calories for 185 lbs), while lighter individuals burn less (e.g., 180 calories for 125 lbs)
Q2: How often should I take breaks when shoveling?
A: Pace yourself and be sure to take frequent breaks. Consider taking a break after 20 to 30 minutes of shoveling, especially when the snow is wet. Consider multiple trips. Consider shoveling periodically throughout the storm to avoid having to move large amounts of snow at once.
Q3: Should you shower after shoveling?

Q4: How often should I take breaks while shoveling snow?
A: Pace yourself and be sure to take frequent breaks. Consider taking a break after 20 to 30 minutes of shoveling, especially when the snow is wet. Consider multiple trips. Consider shoveling periodically throughout the storm to avoid having to move large amounts of snow at once.
Q5: Should you eat before shoveling snow?
A: Drink some water and stretch your arms, shoulders and back. Don't eat a big meal before or soon after shoveling. Eating a large meal can put an extra load on your heart. Use a small shovel or a snow thrower.
Q6: Is it illegal to shovel the road?
A: Shoveling snow into the street is illegal in many places due to the safety risk. It can pose a safety hazard to drivers and cause ice to form. Excessive snow in the street can even cause obstructions that block traffic entirely. Shoveling snow around storm drains can block the flow of ice melt.
