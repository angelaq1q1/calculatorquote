Q1. What is a good trap shooting score?

A: National Shooting Sports Foundation research indicates that the average shooter breaks 13 out of 25 targets on the first try at trapshooting and gradually improves through the high teens into the low 20s. A perfect 25 is a reasonable goal for the novice trap shooter.

Q2. What do "Shots Attempted," "Shots on Target," and "Goals Scored" mean?

A: Shots Attempted means every shot you take. Shots on Target means the shots that go toward the goal and force a save. Goals Scored means the shots that actually go in. These three numbers give you a full view of your shooting accuracy.

Q3. How do I know if my shooting accuracy is good?

A: Your accuracy is good when your Shots on Target percentage is high and steady. Most players aim for 50% or higher, but the "right" number depends on distance and shot type. The best way to judge is to track results over time and watch for improvement.

Q4. Why is my shooting percentage sometimes very high or very low?

A: Your percentage may look high if you only take easy shots. It may look low when you shoot from long distance or under pressure. The most reliable results come from many attempts, not just a single short session.

Q5. Can this calculator help me improve my shooting?

A: Yes. The calculator shows your real performance. When you see your numbers clearly, you can set simple goals and fix weak spots. Tracking your progress makes improvement easier and motivates you to keep practicing.

Q6. Can coaches and teams use this tool?

A: Yes. Coaches can use it during training sessions to track players' accuracy. Teams can record shooting drills, compare positions, and measure progress across games. The tool works for any level, from beginners to advanced players.
