Frequently Asked Questions

What is swim pace and how do I calculate it?
Swim pace is the time it takes to swim a specific distance, typically expressed as time per 100 meters or 100 yards. To calculate swim pace, divide your total time by the distance swam, then multiply by 100. For example, if you swim 400m in 8:00, your pace per 100m is 2:00.

What's the difference between yard pace and meter pace?
Yard pace is slightly faster than meter pace because yards are shorter (1 yard = 0.9144 meters). A 1:30 pace per 100 yards equals approximately 1:38 per 100 meters. Our swimming pace calculator shows both automatically.
What is a good swim pace for beginners?
For beginners, a good swim pace per 100m ranges from 2:30 to 4:00. Competitive swimmers often maintain 1:15-2:00 per 100m, while recreational swimmers typically swim 2:00-3:00 per 100m. Focus on consistency and gradual improvement.
How do I use this for triathlon training?
For triathlon training, use our swim pace calculator to set realistic goals. Olympic distance (1.5km) typically requires 1:45-2:30 per 100m pace. Ironman (3.8km) needs sustainable pacing around 2:00-2:45 per 100m. Practice at race pace during training.
Can I use this calculator for open water swimming?
Yes! While this pace calculator swim tool is based on pool distances, it's perfect for planning open water swims. Remember that open water conditions (currents, waves, temperature) can affect your actual pace, so add 10-20% to your pool pace for realistic open water planning.
