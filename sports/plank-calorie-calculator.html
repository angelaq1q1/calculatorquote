Understand How Plank Calories Are Calculated

Planks are different from running or cycling because your body stays still. You hold one position, so your calorie burn depends on time, weight, and muscle tension. Most fitness studies list the plank at 3.8 METs, which stands for Metabolic Equivalent of Task. The calculator uses a simple and proven formula:

Know What Affects Your Calorie Burn

Different plank styles can change how many calories you burn. A basic forearm plank burns fewer calories than a moving plank, such as plank jacks or a high-plank reach. Good form also affects your result. When you tighten your core, legs, and shoulders, your body works harder and your burn increases. It is also good to remember that planks are not mainly for burning calories. They build strength, improve balance, and support your posture. They work best when you use them with cardio or strength training. A: While planks are excellent for building core strength and muscle tone, they're not the most efficient exercise for burning calories compared to cardio activities. Q5: What is the MET value for plank exercise?
A: The standard MET value for plank exercise is 3.8, which represents a moderate-intensity isometric exercise. This value is based on scientific research and is widely used in fitness and health calculations. Some variations may have slightly different MET values (e.g., 3.3 for light plank, 4.0-4.2 for high-intensity plank), but 3.8 is the most commonly accepted standard value.
