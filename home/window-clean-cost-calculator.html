Window Clean Cost FAQs – Answers to Your Budget & DIY Questions 🪟💦

Q1: What is the best thing to clean your windows with?

A: Use a proper cleaning solution: For an effective and streak-free clean, consider using a white vinegar-based solution. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Wipe in a "Z" pattern: Spray the solution onto the glass and wipe it in a "Z" pattern using a microfibre cloth.

Q2: How do I make my windows crystal clear?

A: The best trick for cleaning windows is using a homemade solution of equal parts water and white vinegar. Spray the mixture on the glass and wipe with a microfiber cloth or squeegee for streak-free results. For tough grime, add a drop of dish soap for extra cleaning power.

Q3: What is the best homemade window cleaner?

A: It's an easy and effective recipe. You'll need: - 1/4 cup distilled white vinegar - 1/2 teaspoon liquid dish soap - 2 cups water - A few drops of your favorite essential oil (Optional) Just add all the ingredients to a spray bottle and shake before using!

Q4: What are the disadvantages of cleaning with vinegar?

A: Vinegar has a very low pH level which means it's very acidic, so it can corrode some surfaces over time. For example, using vinegar on natural stone like granite or marble can dull and scratch the surface and it can corrode surfaces like unsealed grout, window seals, and fridge and dishwasher gaskets.

Q5: Is window cleaning a skilled job?

A: 1. Expertise and Experience. Professional window cleaners possess the skills and experience to tackle even the toughest cleaning jobs. They are trained to handle various types of windows, including hard-to-reach areas that may pose safety risks if attempted by an untrained individual.

Q6: Why are my windows still smeared after cleaning?

A: When you add too much cleaning chemical to a window, it becomes difficult to remove that chemical entirely. Once the window dries and the water evaporates, you are left with chemical residue on the window, which is often the cause of smears.
