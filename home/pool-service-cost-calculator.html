Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: How much is pool service?

A: Pool service costs typically range from $80-$280 per visit, depending on pool size, service type, and frequency. Basic cleaning costs $80-$120, full service $120-$200, and premium service $160-$280. Monthly costs range from $80-$1,120 depending on service frequency and additional services.

Q2: How do I find swimming pool service companies near me?

A: Search online directories like HomeAdvisor, Angi, or Thumbtack for local pool service companies. Check reviews, compare quotes from at least 3 companies, and verify they're licensed and insured. Ask neighbors for recommendations and look for companies with experience in your pool type.

Q3: What qualifications should a swimming pool service technician have?

A: A qualified pool service technician should be certified by organizations like the National Swimming Pool Foundation (NSPF) or Pool & Hot Tub Alliance (PHTA). They should have proper licensing, insurance, and experience with your specific pool type and equipment. Ask about their training and certifications before hiring.

Q4: How much does pool maintenance cost?

A: Pool maintenance costs vary by frequency and service level. Weekly maintenance costs $320-$1,120 monthly, bi-weekly costs $160-$560 monthly, and monthly service costs $80-$280 monthly. Additional factors like pool size, chemical needs, and equipment maintenance can increase costs by 20-50%.

Q5: What does weekly pool service include?

A: Weekly pool service typically includes skimming debris, vacuuming the pool floor, brushing walls and steps, checking and adjusting chemical levels, cleaning skimmer baskets, inspecting equipment, and testing water quality. Some services may also include emptying automatic cleaners and checking pool accessories.
