Q1: What resistor is needed for LEDs?

A: Resistor values depend on the voltage and size of the LEDS. For a 3mm LED on 12 volts, a 1,000 ohm resistor would be a beginning value. Then depending on the desired illumination brightness you may go up or down with the resistor value. Up for dimmer or down for brighter.

Q2: Is a 220 ohm resistor enough for LED?

A: The 220 ohm resistory will work with a 3.3v or 5v feed and cover any color in the bag of LEDs.

Q3: Can I use a 10K resistor for LED?

A: A 10k resistor is frequently used in LED circuits to limit current and prevent damage.

Q4: What happens if you run an LED without a resistor?

A: Yes. LEDs will draw enough power to burn themselves out if not limited by a resistor.

Q5: What size resistor for LED 5v?

A: The circuit is powered with 5 volts which means that the current limiting resistor should be 150 ohms or higher. This ensures that the max current will not exceed 20mA. The resistor values used are 300 ohms, 1K, 2K 10K, and 100K. The voltage drop across each LED is the same and is around 1.9 volts.

Q6: How much does a 10k resistor reduce voltage?

A: Ohm's law allows you to calculate the voltage drop across a given resistor if you know the current flowing through the resistor. Example: How much voltage is dropped across a 10K ohm resistor which has 5mA flowing through it? The voltage dropped across the resistor is 50 volts, because E = IR = 5mA * 10K = 50 volts.
