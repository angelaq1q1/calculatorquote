Frequently Asked Questions About Carpet Cleaning

Q1: How can I deep clean my carpet myself?

A: First, vacuum slowly in different directions to lift as much dirt as possible, then spot-treat stains with a mild solution (like white vinegar and water or a carpet-safe cleaner). If you can, rent or borrow a carpet cleaning machine for hot water extraction. It's the closest to what professionals use.

Q2: What is the best way to clean a carpet?

A: Steam Cleaning. The heat and moisture help to break down dirt and grime, which is then extracted by the machine. Steam cleaning can be very effective for deep cleaning, and it's a method often recommended by carpet manufacturers.

Q3: Does baking soda really clean carpets?

A: Baking soda is great for cleaning and deodorizing carpet, and it's also great to clean multiple areas throughout your home.

Q4: What should you not clean carpet with?

A: Bleach might be able to remove a carpet stain, but it's likely to leave a bleach stain in its place. White wine is said to remove red wine stains from the carpet, but it's likely to leave a new stain. Laundry Detergent. Abrasive Cleaners. Dish Soap.

Q5: Is it worth professionally cleaning a carpet?

A: Having a carpet regularly cleaned by professionals will help to prolong its lifespan, and prevent problems becoming untreatable issues, so it's well worth getting into the cleaning habit early on when buying a new carpet.

Q6: Is it better to vacuum before or after shampooing?

A: By eliminating surface dirt, carpet cleaning equipment can focus on deep-seated dirt and stains within the carpet fibers. Vacuuming beforehand ensures cleaning agents can penetrate the fibers and reach and break down dirt, stains, and non-living allergens embedded in the carpet.
