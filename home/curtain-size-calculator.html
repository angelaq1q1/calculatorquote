How Curtain Sizes Are Calculated and Used

Most people ask one simple question: what is the standard curtain size? For window curtains, common standard lengths (in inches) are: 24, 36, 72, 84, 96, and 108. Short curtains (24 and 36 inches) are often used above sinks, radiators, or in bathrooms. Long curtains (72+ inches) are used for full window coverage.

The calculator works by matching your window measurements with your preferred curtain style and fullness. A standard window usually works with floor-length curtains. A taller ceiling may need a longer curtain for better coverage. A wider window may need extra width for full coverage.

Important Tips to Avoid Common Mistakes

There are a few things every user should watch for. For window curtains, you should always measure from the top of the window frame to where you want the curtain to stop. For floor-length curtains, measure to the floor. Measure window width from the left outer edge to the right outer edge. Consider placing the rod higher than the window for a taller appearance. For safety, keep curtains above heat sources like fireplaces or radiators.

For shower curtains, you should always measure the rod, not the tub. You should also allow extra width so the curtain can fold naturally. A tight curtain does not block water well. Fabric type also matters. Heavier materials hang differently than plastic liners. By understanding these points, you avoid buying the wrong size and returning it later.

A: What is standard shower curtain size? The standard shower curtain size is 72 x 72 inches. Standard shower curtain length extends to the floor and covers the tub.

Q2: Are all shower curtains 72?

A: 72" is the standard length for most shower curtains while 84" is the standard length for longer tubs and showers.

Q3: What to avoid in a shower curtain?

A: Beyond PVC and phthalates, traditional plastic shower curtains may contain an array of additional toxic chemicals. These can include lead, mercury, as well as various stabilizers and colorants.

A: As a best practice, consider washing your shower curtains at least once a month. With daily usage in your home, shower curtains and liners can quickly become soiled, but by regularly hand washing or running through your washing machine can help keep them fresh and clean.
