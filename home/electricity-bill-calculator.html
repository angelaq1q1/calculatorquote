Electricity Bill Questions: Answers to Common Concerns

Q1. What is the meaning of electricity bill?
A: An electric bill, also commonly referred to as an electricity bill or power bill, is a statement issued by an electricity provider detailing the amount of electricity consumed by a consumer (individual, household, or business) over a specific period, typically a month.

Q2. What is another name for the electric bill?
A: The conversation revolves around what people call their utility bills, with various terms being used such as electric bill, light bill, power bill, and energy bill, depending on the location and personal preference.

Q3. What costs most in an electricity bill?
A: Let's start with what uses the most electricity in your home ‚Äî the air conditioner and heater. Heating and cooling account for a whopping 32.1% of your home's electricity consumption.

Q4. How do I know my electric bill?
A: There are two ways to view your electronic bills.
1. Via email notification. Click the link to your electric bill in the bill notification sent to your registered email address.
2. Via Meralco Online. Log in to your My Meralco Account and access the View Bills facility.

Q5. What uses the most electricity at home?
A: Which home appliances use the most electricity?
1. Heating and cooling: 45-50% The largest electricity consumer in the average household is your heating and cooling appliance.
2. Water heater: 12%
3. Lighting: 9-12%
4. Refrigerator: 8%
5. Washer and dryer: 5%
6. Electric oven: 3%
7. Dishwasher: 2%
8. TV and cable box: 2%

Q6. How do they calculate the electricity bill?
A: Bills are built by taking readings in order to convert them into kilowatt hours (also known as units, or shortened to kWh), which is then used to establish your charge. A kilowatt (KW) is 1,000 watts of electricity. A kilowatt hour (kWh) is 1,000 watts of electricity consumed for 1 hour.
