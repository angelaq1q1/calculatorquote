‚ùì FAQ - Solar Savings Calculator Questions

Q1: How much money will I save with solar panels?
A: Solar savings vary significantly by location and energy usage. A typical 6kW system can save $1,200-$3,600 annually, with total 25-year savings ranging from $30,000 to $90,000. Use our solar calculator with your specific electricity bill and location for accurate estimates.

Q2: What is the average payback period for solar panels?
A: Solar panel payback periods typically range from 6 to 12 years, depending on your electricity rates and available incentives. In high-rate states like California and Hawaii, payback can be as short as 4-6 years. Lower-rate states may take 8-12 years to break even.

Q3: How do I calculate solar panel ROI?
A: Solar ROI is calculated by dividing your total 25-year savings by your net installation cost (after incentives). Most residential solar systems achieve 200-400% ROI over 25 years. Our calculator automatically computes this based on your location, energy usage, and system specifications.

Q4: Should I pay cash or finance my solar panels?
A: Cash purchases typically provide 10-15% higher ROI due to avoiding loan interest. However, solar loans allow you to start saving immediately while paying monthly. Compare both options in our calculator to see which works better for your financial situation.

Q5: How much do solar panels cost to install?
A: Solar panel cost varies by location and system size, typically ranging from $2.70-$4.00 per watt. A 6kW system costs $16,200-$24,000 before incentives. Use our detailed cost calculator to get precise estimates for your home.

Q6: How do electricity rate increases affect my solar savings?
A: Electricity rates historically increase 2-3% annually. As rates rise, your solar savings grow proportionally. A $150 monthly bill today could be $300+ in 15 years, making your solar savings much more valuable over time. Our calculator factors in these increases for realistic long-term projections.

Q7: What's the difference between annual savings and 25-year savings?
A: Annual savings show your immediate yearly benefit, while 25-year savings include the cumulative effect of electricity rate increases and represent your total financial gain over the system's lifetime. The 25-year figure is typically 2-3 times higher than simple annual savings multiplied by 25.
