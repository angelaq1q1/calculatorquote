House Cleaning Calculator FAQ — Everything You Need to Know
Q1. How much can a cleaner do in 3 hours?
A: Decluttering, dusting, vacuuming, and wet mopping are some core tasks that cleaners can complete within these three hours.
Q2. What does a typical house cleaning consist of?
A: Standard Room-by-Room Cleaning. During each room cleaning, the cleaners will focus on dusting and wiping down all surfaces, including furniture, shelves, picture frames and more. They'll also vacuum or sweep and mop the floors, taking care to remove any debris or build-up in corners or under furniture.
Q3. What are the 7 stages of cleaning?
A: The 7 Stages Of Effective Cleaning Explained: Step 1: Remove debris. The first step in the cleaning process is removing visible debris signs. Step 2: Rinse. Use high-pressure warm water to rinse away debris. Step 3: Apply detergent. Step 4: Inspect. Step 5: Sanitise. Step 6: Disinfect. Step 7: Leave to dry.
Q4. What are the 4 principles of cleaning?
A: The four principles of cleaning are time, temperature, chemistry, and mechanics. These rules work together to help remove dirt and germs effectively. Cleaning differs from sanitizing, disinfecting, and sterilizing. Each process has its own role in keeping homes safe by reducing or eliminating microbes on surfaces.
Q5. How do you calculate cleaning costs?
A: Square footage typically translates directly to cleaning time, with homes under 1,000 sq ft taking around 2-4 hours, while larger homes (e.g., 2,000-3,000 sq ft) can require 5-8 hours or more. A general guideline is to charge around $0.05 to $0.10 per square foot, depending on the level of detail required.
Q6. Which part of the house takes you the longest to clean?
A: Quick Cleaning Timing Summary: Kitchen: 45–60 mins — grease and appliance buildup. Bathroom: 40–50 mins — grime and tile grout. Living Room: 30 mins — dust and upholstery. Bedroom: 25 mins — clutter and organization. Outdoor Area: 60+ mins — weather wear and debris.
