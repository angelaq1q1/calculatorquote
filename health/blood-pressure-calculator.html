    Understanding Your Blood Pressure: What the Numbers Mean

    Blood pressure uses two simple numbers. The systolic number shows the pressure when your heart beats. The diastolic number shows the pressure when your heart rests. This calculator uses a max-value rule. Your result follows the higher category between the two numbers. If your systolic is normal but your diastolic is high, the higher group decides the final category.

    Q1: What is a normal blood pressure reading? üìä

    A: A normal blood pressure reading is systolic pressure less than 120 mmHg and diastolic pressure less than 80 mmHg (written as <120/80). This is considered optimal for cardiovascular health and indicates your heart and blood vessels are working efficiently.

    Q2: What does elevated blood pressure mean?

    A: Elevated blood pressure (also called prehypertension) is when systolic pressure is 120-129 mmHg and diastolic is less than 80 mmHg. This indicates you're at risk for developing hypertension and should take preventive measures like reducing sodium intake, increasing exercise, and maintaining a healthy weight.

    Q3: What is Stage 1 hypertension?

    A: Stage 1 hypertension is when systolic pressure is 130-139 mmHg or diastolic pressure is 80-89 mmHg. This requires lifestyle changes and may need medication depending on your overall cardiovascular risk. Regular monitoring and consultation with a healthcare provider is recommended to prevent progression to Stage 2.

    Q4: What is Stage 2 hypertension?

    A: Stage 2 hypertension is when systolic pressure is 140 mmHg or higher, or diastolic pressure is 90 mmHg or higher. This requires immediate medical attention, lifestyle changes, and typically medication to manage. Untreated Stage 2 hypertension significantly increases risk of heart attack, stroke, and other complications.

    Q5: What is a hypertensive crisis?

    A: A hypertensive crisis is a medical emergency when systolic pressure exceeds 180 mmHg or diastolic pressure exceeds 120 mmHg. This requires immediate medical attention as it can cause organ damage, stroke, or heart attack. If you experience this reading, seek emergency care immediately. üö®

    Q6: How often should I check my blood pressure?

    A: If you have normal blood pressure, check it annually. If you have elevated or Stage 1 hypertension, check it monthly or as recommended by your doctor. For Stage 2 or higher, follow your healthcare provider's monitoring schedule, which may be weekly or daily. Regular monitoring helps track changes and treatment effectiveness.
