Q2: Does infant length predict height?

A: A baby's size at birth doesn't reliably predict their height as an adult. Genetics has the biggest influence on a child's future height.

Q3: Does baby weight correlate with height?

A: There was a strong positive association between birth weight and adult height; for subjects with birth weight < or = 2,500 g, mean height was 175.7 cm, while for those with birth weight > or = 4,501 g, mean height was 184.1 cm. A positive association was also found between birth length and adult height.

Q4: When should I worry about baby weight?

A: Slow weight gain could be a problem if: Your newborn doesn't regain their birth weight within 10 to 14 days after their birth. Your baby up to 3 months old gains less than an ounce a day. Your infant between 3 and 6 months gains less than 0.67 ounces a day.

Q5: Which parent do babies get their height from?

A: The best predictor of a child's height is their parents' height or, more specifically, the mid-parental height. The mid-parental height is calculated by adding the mother's and father's height, adding 13 cm (5 inches) for boys or subtracting 13 cm (5 inches) for girls, and then finally dividing by 2.
