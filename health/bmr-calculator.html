Understanding Your TDEE Values

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) shows how many calories you burn per day based on your activity level. Each value range represents the calories needed to maintain your current weight for that level of activity:

  • Sedentary (little to no exercise): Minimal movement; calories represent what your body needs at rest plus everyday activities.
  • Lightly Active (light exercise 1-3 days/week): Slightly higher calories to account for occasional workouts or light daily activity.
  • Moderately Active (moderate exercise 3-5 days/week): Reflects regular exercise or a moderately active lifestyle.
  • Very Active (hard exercise 6-7 days/week): Includes intense daily workouts or physically demanding work.
  • Extremely Active (very hard exercise, physical job): Represents calories for heavy manual labor or very high-intensity daily training.

How to use these numbers:

  • Eating slightly fewer calories than your TDEE promotes weight loss.
  • Eating around your TDEE maintains your current weight.
  • Eating more calories than your TDEE supports muscle gain.
