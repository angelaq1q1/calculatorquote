How the Calculator Works: Easy, Personalized Assessment

The calculator checks the main risk factors of tooth decay. First, it looks at your daily oral hygiene. Brushing too little or skipping flossing can raise your risk. Next, it examines your diet. Eating sweets often increases your chances of cavities. Using xylitol reduces risk of tooth decay by fighting cavity-causing bacteria.

Lifestyle habits also matter. For example, breathing through your mouth during sleep may increase tooth decay risk because it dries saliva, which protects teeth naturally. Smoking or low saliva flow can also increase risk. The calculator considers your age, dental history, and dentist visits. Fluoride is another key factor in preventing tooth decay.

Each answer gets a point value. The calculator totals the points and shows your risk level: low, moderate, or high. Low risk means good habits. Moderate risk means you should improve your routine. High risk means you should visit a dentist soon. This system helps you see how your choices affect your teeth and guides you to easy preventive steps.

Q1: What are the risks of tooth decay?

A: Pain that makes it hard to live your daily life. Weight loss or nutrition problems because it's painful or difficult to eat or chew. Tooth loss, which may affect your appearance, confidence and self-esteem. In rare cases, a tooth abscess, which can lead to more-serious or even life-threatening infections.

Q2: What are the 5 stages of tooth decay?

A: Tooth Decay Stages: 1: Demineralization. This is the first sign of tooth decay that shows up on the surface of the tooth (enamel). 2: Enamel Decay. If tooth decay isn't stopped then it will continue to further erode the enamel. 3: Dentin Decay. 4: Pulp Damage. 5: Abscess.

Q3: Can a tooth with decay be saved?

A: Most cavities can be treated with a filling if you see a dentist in time. A more severely decayed tooth may need a larger restoration, such as a crown or root canal therapy, or it may need to be removed. Tooth decay can be prevented by maintaining a good daily oral hygiene routine and your regular dental visits.

Q4: What kills tooth decay naturally?

A: Use fluoride. Fluoride is a mineral that can prevent tooth decay from progressing. It can even reverse or stop early tooth decay. Fluoride works to protect teeth.

Q5: Can vitamin D deficiency cause cavities?

A: Vitamin D plays a key role in absorbing and retaining calcium and phosphorous for bone and tooth mineralization. When your vitamin D levels are unregulated, it weakens your teeth, making you highly susceptible to cavities, fractures, and decay.

Q6: How often should you brush to prevent decay?

A: Brush your teeth with fluoride toothpaste twice a day for about 2 minutes to help keep your teeth and mouth healthy. Plaque is a film of bacteria that coats your teeth if you don't brush them properly. It contributes to gum disease and tooth decay. Tooth brushing stops plaque building up.
