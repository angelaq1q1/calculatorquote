Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is menstrual cycle calculated?

A: A menstrual cycle is calculated from the first day of your period to the day before your next period starts. This includes both the bleeding days and the days in between periods.

Q: How often should your menstrual cycle be?

A: A normal menstrual cycle typically occurs every 21-35 days, with 28 days being the average. Most women have cycles that are consistent within a few days each month.

Q: How to calculate menstrual cycle length?

A: To calculate your cycle length, count the days from the first day of one period to the day before your next period starts. Track this for 3-6 months to get your average cycle length.

Q: How many days between each menstrual cycle?

A: The days between periods (from the end of one period to the start of the next) typically range from 16-30 days, depending on your total cycle length and period duration.

Q: How frequently should your period be?

A: Your period should occur every 21-35 days. If your cycles are consistently shorter than 21 days or longer than 35 days, it's recommended to consult with a healthcare provider.

Q: Does cycle length include period?

A: Yes, cycle length includes your period days. The cycle starts on the first day of your period and ends the day before your next period begins, including all the bleeding days.

Q: Does 28 day cycle include period?

A: Yes, a 28-day cycle includes the period. For example, if your period lasts 5 days, you would have 5 days of bleeding followed by 23 days before your next period starts.
