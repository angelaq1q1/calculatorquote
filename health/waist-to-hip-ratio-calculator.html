Q: Why is waist-hip ratio important?

A: The waist-to-hip ratio (WHR) is an important health indicator because it measures the distribution of body fat. A higher WHR indicates more abdominal fat, which is associated with increased health risks including cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and metabolic syndrome. Unlike BMI, WHR specifically identifies where fat is stored in your body, making it a more accurate predictor of health risks related to obesity.

Q: How should I measure waist and hips?

A: Waist measurement: Stand straight and measure around the narrowest part of your waist, usually just above the belly button. Don't suck in your stomach or pull the tape too tight.
Hip measurement: Measure around the widest part of your hips and buttocks. The tape should be parallel to the floor and snug but not tight. For best results, measure in the morning before eating, wear minimal clothing, and use a flexible measuring tape.

Q: How do I calculate the waist to hip ratio?

A: The waist-to-hip ratio is calculated by dividing your waist circumference by your hip circumference. The formula is: WHR = Waist Circumference √∑ Hip Circumference. For example, if your waist is 80 cm and your hips are 100 cm, your WHR would be 0.8. The ratio is expressed as a decimal number, and the interpretation depends on your gender and the resulting value.
