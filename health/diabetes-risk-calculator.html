It's also useful to understand the different kinds of diabetes. There are 4 types of diabetes that most people know about: Type 1, Type 2, gestational, and secondary diabetes caused by other health problems. Some doctors describe even more detailed categories — sometimes called the 7 types of diabetes — to show how causes and symptoms can vary. No matter which type, the goal is the same: to detect it early and make healthy changes before it gets worse 🌿. Our calculator helps you take that first small but important step.

Diabetes Risk FAQs 💉 — Your Top Questions Answered

Q1: What is the risk of diabetes?

A: Diabetes majorly increases the risk of many heart problems. These can include coronary artery disease with chest pain (angina), heart attack, stroke and narrowing of arteries (atherosclerosis). If you have diabetes, you're more likely to have heart disease or stroke. Nerve damage from diabetes (diabetic neuropathy).

Q2: Can a person with diabetes live a healthy life?

A: People with diabetes can be active, even if they take insulin or use technology such as insulin pumps. Try to do different kinds of activities. While being more active may have more health benefits, any physical activity is better than none. Start slowly with activities you enjoy.

Q3: What to avoid if you have diabetes?

A: The foods and drinks you should limit include: High-carb foods and drinks, such as: Sugary foods, such as candy, cookies, cake, ice cream, sweetened cereals, and canned fruits with added sugar. Drinks with added sugars, such as juice, regular soda, and regular sports or energy drinks.

Q4: Can you live 100 years with diabetes?

A: The younger a person who receives a type 2 diabetes diagnosis is, the higher the risk that diabetes-linked complications will shorten their lifespan. However, by adopting effective management strategies, there is a good chance that a person living with type 2 diabetes may live as long as a person without the condition.

Q5: What are the big 3 signs of diabetes?

A: The three Ps are the most common signs of diabetes. Polyuria means frequent urination, polydipsia means excessive thirst, and polyphagia means excessive hunger.

Q6: What is a diabetic belly?

A: Fat around your middle that goes deeper into your abdomen is called visceral fat, and it can collect around your internal organs. It has been called "diabetes belly" because having this kind of fat may increase your risk of developing type 2 diabetes, and it's more common in people who have type 2 diabetes.
