❓ FAQ – because we know what you're thinking

Q: What is an anti-inflammatory diet?

A: An anti-inflammatory diet is a way of eating that focuses on foods that help reduce chronic inflammation in the body. This diet emphasizes whole, unprocessed foods like fruits, vegetables, fatty fish, nuts, seeds, and whole grains while limiting processed foods, refined sugars, and unhealthy fats that can promote inflammation.

Q: How to start an anti-inflammatory diet?

A: To start an anti-inflammatory diet, begin by gradually incorporating more anti-inflammatory foods into your meals. Start with adding 2-3 servings of vegetables daily, include fatty fish 2-3 times per week, and replace refined grains with whole grains. Gradually reduce processed foods, sugary drinks, and red meat while increasing your water intake.

Q: What can I eat on an anti-inflammatory diet?

A: On an anti-inflammatory diet, you can enjoy: leafy greens (spinach, kale), colorful vegetables (broccoli, bell peppers), fatty fish (salmon, mackerel), nuts and seeds (almonds, walnuts, chia seeds), whole grains (oats, brown rice), fruits (berries, oranges), olive oil, and green tea. These foods are rich in antioxidants and omega-3 fatty acids that combat inflammation.

Q: How to start anti-inflammatory diet for beginners?

A: For beginners, start with small, manageable changes: replace one processed meal per day with a whole food option, add one serving of vegetables to each meal, and gradually increase your water intake. Use our calculator to assess your current diet and get personalized recommendations. Focus on consistency rather than perfection, and remember that even small changes can make a significant difference in reducing inflammation.
