Q1: What is the normal healthy weight?

A: If your BMI is: Under 18.5 - you are very underweight and possibly malnourished. 18.5 to 24.9 - you have a healthy weight range for young and middle-aged adults. 25.0 to 29.9 - you are overweight.

Q2: How can I reduce weight?

A: One of the best ways to lose body fat is through steady aerobic exercise, such as brisk walking. Work up to at least 30 minutes of aerobic exercise most days of the week. Some people may need more exercise than this to lose weight and keep it off. Also aim to do strength training exercises at least twice a week.

Q3: What age is harder to lose weight?

A: If you're an adult in the U.S., you can expect to gain 10 to 25 pounds between your 20s and your 40s. Starting between ages 30 and 40, you may find losing weight and exercising more challenging. The exercise you do may not have the same effect as before. It's not necessarily a sign that something is wrong.

Q4: What is a good chest size?

A: If your chest doesn't measure over 110 cm, don't stress. Most men fall somewhere between 100 and 110 cm, depending on factors like height, frame, muscle mass, and body fat levels. And remember: chest circumference isn't just about pec size.

Q5: How to lose 5 kg in a week?

A: Reduce intake of processed, high-calorie foods. Avoid overeating by practicing portion control. Stay adequately hydrated by drinking enough water daily. Engage in moderate-intensity aerobic exercise for at least 120-150 minutes per week

Q6: Which body part loses fat first?

A: People often lose weight in different parts of the body first, depending on their individual physiology. So if you're looking to lose weight quickly, focus on reducing your calorie intake and exercising regularly ‚Äì you may see results in your face and neck before anywhere else!
