Q1: What is a healthy weight?

A: A healthy weight is a weight range that's considered optimal for your body type based on your height, age, and sex. It's typically determined using BMI (Body Mass Index) guidelines, with a healthy BMI range of 18.5-25 kg/mÂ².

Q2: How is healthy weight calculated?

A: Healthy weight is calculated using BMI guidelines. The healthy weight range is determined by applying the healthy BMI range (18.5-25) to your height. This gives you a weight range in kilograms and pounds that's considered optimal for your body type.

Q3: Why is maintaining a healthy weight important?

A: Maintaining a healthy weight reduces the risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, joint problems, sleep disorders, and other health complications. It also improves overall quality of life and energy levels.

Q4: Is BMI the only factor in determining healthy weight?

A: While BMI is a useful screening tool, it doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or body composition. Other factors like waist circumference, body fat percentage, and overall health should also be considered when determining your ideal weight.
