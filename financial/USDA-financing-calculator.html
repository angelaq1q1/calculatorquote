What Are USDA Mortgage Interest Rates?

USDA mortgage interest rates are usually lower than regular home loans or FHA loans. These rates stay the same for the life of your loan and come from approved lenders, but the U.S. Department of Agriculture backs them up. Your rate might be different depending on your credit score and how much debt you have compared to your income. Use our calculator above to see what your monthly payments might look like with a typical USDA loan rate.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: How much USDA loan can I get?
A: It depends on your income, how many people live in your house, where the property is, and how much debt you have. Our USDA loan calculator gives you an estimate based on your situation.
Q2: What does a USDA loan calculator do?
A: It helps you figure out your monthly payments, shows you the guarantee fees, and tells you if you might qualify for rural development loans. It's a tool to calculate USDA mortgage payments including taxes and insurance.
Q3: Does your USDA loan calculator include taxes and insurance?
A: Yes! Our USDA loan calculator with taxes and insurance shows you everything you'll pay each month - your loan payment, property taxes, home insurance, and USDA guarantee fees.
Q4: What are USDA guarantee fees and how do you figure them?
A: There's a 1% upfront fee that gets added to your loan amount, and a 0.35% yearly fee that gets added to your monthly payment. Our calculator puts these in automatically so you see the real cost.
Q5: Can I use your USDA loan calculator for farm loans?
A: Yes! Our USDA farm loan calculator works for both houses and farm properties. It handles USDA direct loan payment calculator needs and guaranteed loan estimates.
Q6: What's the difference between USDA direct and guaranteed loan calculators?
A: USDA direct loans come straight from the government, while guaranteed loans come from private banks but the government backs them. Our calculator works for both types and gives you accurate USDA loan estimates.
