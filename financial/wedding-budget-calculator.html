Wedding Budget FAQs: Everything You Need to Know

Q1: How do I calculate my wedding budget?

A: Wedding Budget Breakdown:

  1. RECEPTION VENUE, CATERING, & RENTALS (45%) 🏰
  2. WEDDING PLANNER/COORDINATOR (5-15%) 📋
  3. CEREMONY (3%) 💒
  4. PHOTOGRAPHY AND/OR VIDEOGRAPHY (12%) 📸
  5. STATIONERY (3%) 📝
  6. WEDDING ATTIRE & BEAUTY (9%) 👗
  7. WEDDING RINGS (2%) 💍
  8. CAKE (2%) 🎂

Q2: What is the ideal budget for a wedding?

A: The average Indian wedding cost typically ranges between Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 50 lakh, making financial planning essential. Whether you are planning an intimate gathering or a lavish affair, understanding the average wedding cost in India is crucial for effective financial planning. 💰

Q3: What is a realistic budget for a 100 person wedding?

A: The average cost of a midsize (100 person) wedding in North America is about $25-30k, based on what I've read when I was looking to set our budget. Depending on where you live and size of wedding that cost could be more or less. That average seems about right to me. 📊

Q4: What is the cheapest month to get married?

A: The cheapest months to get married are January, December and November. Late fall through all through winter and into early spring are considered the off-season for weddings. Only 23% of weddings happened between November and March last year, according to The Knot 2025 Real Weddings Study. ❄️

Q5: What is the most expensive part of the wedding?

A: The venue is often the most expensive part of a wedding. Whether you're hosting your ceremony, reception, or both, the venue sets the stage for your entire event. Costs can vary widely depending on location, size, and the time of year. 🏛️

Q6: Who pays for a wedding?

A: According to traditional wedding etiquette, the bride's family assumes most of the financial costs associated with a wedding, including the wedding planner, invitations, dress, ceremony, reception, flowers, photography, and music. 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
