Q2: What is the meaning of compound interest?

A: Compound interest is the interest earned on both your initial deposit (principal) and the accumulated interest from previous periods. This creates a "snowball effect" where your money grows exponentially over time because the interest earned also starts to earn interest. It differs from simple interest, which is calculated only on the original principal.
