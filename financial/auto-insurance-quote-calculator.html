Q1. Who usually has the cheapest car insurance?
A: State Farm is the cheapest large auto insurance company in the nation for liability coverage, with an average rate of $40 a month and $485 a year, according to NerdWallet's October 2025 analysis. Most states require a certain amount of liability coverage to legally drive.
Q2. Which is the most cheapest car insurance?
A: Third party cover is the minimum level required by law and normally the cheapest cover available. It only covers you for damaging someone else's car (or property) or injuring another person (a 'third party') in an accident. It won't cover any injuries to you or damage to your car. Q5. Do I need both comprehensive and third party insurance?
A: If your car is damaged in the same event, and the accident is your fault, it won't be covered for repairs. So, if the value of your car isn't huge, third party car insurance may be enough cover for you. But if you think you'll need to cover your vehicle, comprehensive car insurance might be worth considering üîç.
