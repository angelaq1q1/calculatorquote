About Army Retirement

Types of Army Retirement Systems

Our comprehensive Army Retirement Calculator serves multiple purposes for different types of service members. Whether you're looking for a Reserve Army Retirement Calculator, an Army Retirement Calculator High 3, or a general US Army Retirement Calculator, our tool provides accurate calculations for all retirement scenarios.

Legacy Retirement System (High-3)

The Legacy system, also known as the Army Retirement Calculator High 3, calculates retirement pay based on the average of your highest 36 months of basic pay. The formula is: Years of Service × 2.5% × Average of Highest 36 Months of Basic Pay.

Blended Retirement System (BRS)

The BRS combines a reduced pension (Years of Service × 2% × Average of Highest 36 Months of Basic Pay) with a Thrift Savings Plan (TSP) and continuation pay.

Key Factors Affecting Retirement Pay

  • Years of Service: More years generally mean higher retirement pay
  • Rank at Retirement: Higher rank means higher base pay
  • Retirement Plan: Legacy vs. BRS affects calculations
  • Disability: Can affect tax treatment of retirement pay
  • Survivor Benefits: SBP reduces retirement pay but provides survivor protection

Additional Benefits

  • VA Disability: Tax-free compensation for service-connected disabilities
  • TRICARE: Healthcare coverage for retirees and families
  • Commissary/Exchange: Access to military shopping facilities
  • Space-A Travel: Space-available travel on military aircraft

Tax Considerations

Military retirement pay is generally subject to federal income tax, but some states don't tax military retirement. VA disability pay is tax-free. Understanding your tax situation is crucial for accurate retirement planning.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How much is a 20 year Army pension worth?

A 20-year Army pension under the Legacy system equals 50% of your base pay (20 years × 2.5%). For example, if your base pay is $5,000/month, your monthly pension would be $2,500. Under BRS, it would be 40% of base pay ($2,000/month) plus TSP benefits.

What is the average Army retirement pay?

The average Army retirement pay varies by rank and years of service. A typical E-7 with 20 years might receive around $2,500-$3,500 monthly, while an O-5 with 20 years could receive $5,000-$7,000 monthly. Use our calculator for your specific situation.

How is Army retirement pay calculated?

Army retirement pay is calculated using the formula: Years of Service × Multiplier × Average of Highest 36 Months of Basic Pay. The multiplier is 2.5% for Legacy and 2% for BRS.

What's the difference between Legacy and BRS?

Legacy provides a higher pension (2.5% multiplier) but no government TSP contributions. BRS offers a lower pension (2% multiplier) plus TSP matching and continuation pay.

Is Army retirement pay taxable?

Yes, Army retirement pay is generally subject to federal income tax. However, some states don't tax military retirement, and VA disability pay is tax-free.

When can I start receiving Army retirement pay?

You can start receiving Army retirement pay immediately upon retirement if you have 20+ years of service, or at age 60 if you have less than 20 years but qualify for early retirement.

How does the Reserve Army Retirement Calculator work?

What is the Army Retirement Calculator High 3 system?

The Army Retirement Calculator High 3 refers to the Legacy retirement system that calculates your pension based on the average of your highest 36 months of basic pay. This system provides a 2.5% multiplier for each year of service, making it the most generous retirement option for long-term service members.
