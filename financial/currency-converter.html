Q6: What is a floating currency?
A: A floating exchange rate is an exchange rate system where a country's currency price is determined by the foreign exchange market, depending on the relative supply and demand of other currencies. A floating exchange rate is not restrained by trade limits or government controls, unlike a fixed exchange rate.
