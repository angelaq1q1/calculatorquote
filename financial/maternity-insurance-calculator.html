‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions About Maternity Insurance

Q1. What does maternity insurance cover?

A: Maternity insurance covers maternity-related hospitalisation up to 30 days before birth and 60 days after delivery. Also, delivery expenses, including pre-and post-natal fees, and hospitalisation charges. Maternity health insurance also covers your newborn baby. You get coverage for normal and C-section deliveries.

Q2. What is the minimum waiting period for maternity insurance?

A: It is at least a minimum of 30 days. The maternity cover waiting period is the time between the end of the initial waiting period and the start of the coverage for maternity expenses. This period usually lasts for 9 months to 6 years.

Q3. Can I get insurance if I am already pregnant?

A: Yes. However, while you are still pregnant, you may only enroll during an Open Enrollment period, which is typically from November through mid-January. Once enrolled, your plan will be required to cover maternity services.

Q5. What if I'm pregnant but don't have insurance?

A: If you report your pregnancy, you may be eligible for free or low-cost coverage through Medicaid or the Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP). If you're found eligible for Medicaid or CHIP, your information will be sent to the state agency, and you won't be given the option to keep your Marketplace plan.

Q6. How to claim maternity benefit?

A: Fill in a Maternity Benefit application form (MB1) and send it to the Maternity Benefit Section of the Department of Social Protection. The form includes an MB2 section which must be completed by your employer. If you're not working or you are self-employed, you must get section of the form completed by your doctor.
